# Stock Trader Skill

This skill provides the procedural knowledge and frameworks to identify undervalued stocks and make predictions based on historical financial facts and fundamental analysis. It covers finding potential "next big stocks," performing deep due diligence, and calculating intrinsic value.

## 1. Core Workflow: Value-Based Stock Selection

To spot the next big undervalued stock, follow this rigorous research sequence:

1. **Discovery**: Use stock screeners to filter for companies with low valuation multiples (P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA) and strong historical growth.
2. **Fundamental Deep Dive**: Analyze the last 5–10 years of financial statements (10-K, 10-Q) to verify revenue consistency, debt levels, and cash flow health.
3. **Moat Assessment**: Evaluate the company's competitive advantage (e.g., patents, brand, cost structure) to ensure its future profitability is protected.
4. **Intrinsic Value Calculation**: Use methods like DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) or Historical Multiples to determine the stock's "fair value."
5. **Catalyst Identification**: Identify upcoming events (e.g., product launches, earnings reports, industry shifts) that could act as a trigger for the stock's price to reach its fair value.

## 2. Fact-Based Prediction Principles

Predictions must be grounded in historical data and financial reality, not hype:

- **Mean Reversion**: Historically, stock prices tend to revert to their long-term average valuation multiples. Use this to predict price targets.
- **Insider Alignment**: Significant buying by company executives is a powerful historical indicator of future outperformance.
- **Institutional Accumulation**: Increasing ownership by top-tier hedge funds and pension funds often precedes major stock breakouts.
- **Safety Margin**: Only predict success if the current price offers a significant "margin of safety" (e.g., 20–30% below intrinsic value).

## 4. Professional Research Toolset

Efficiency and accuracy are critical in fundamental analysis. Use these tools for their respective purposes:

- **Yahoo Finance / Google Finance**: For quick access to historical prices and basic financial data.
- **Finviz**: For powerful stock screening based on fundamental and technical criteria.
- **Seeking Alpha**: For in-depth analyst reports and earnings call transcripts.
- **SEC EDGAR**: For accessing official 10-K and 10-Q filings for raw financial data.
- **Morningstar**: For independent ratings on a company's competitive "moat" and management quality.
- **TradingView**: For analyzing long-term price trends and historical support/resistance levels.
