# Stock Valuation and Prediction Framework

## 1. Core Valuation Metrics (Spotting Undervalued Stocks)

To identify if a stock is trading below its intrinsic value, analyze these key ratios compared to historical averages and industry peers:

- **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**: Compares share price to earnings per share. A low P/E relative to historical norms or peers may indicate undervaluation.
- **Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio**: Compares market value to book value. A ratio below 1.0 often suggests the stock is undervalued or the company has fundamental issues.
- **EV/EBITDA**: Enterprise Value to Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. Useful for comparing companies with different debt levels.
- **Free Cash Flow (FCF) Yield**: Measures the cash a company generates relative to its market value. High FCF yield is a strong indicator of value.
- **PEG Ratio**: P/E divided by earnings growth rate. A PEG below 1.0 is a classic sign of an undervalued growth stock.

## 2. Fact-Based Prediction (Historical Analysis)

Predictions must be grounded in historical performance and fundamental shifts:

- **Earnings Consistency**: Analyze the last 5–10 years of EPS (Earnings Per Share) growth. Consistent growth is a predictor of future stability.
- **Revenue Trends**: Look for accelerating revenue growth over multiple quarters, which often precedes a stock breakout.
- **Debt-to-Equity (D/E)**: Evaluate if the company is over-leveraged. Historically, companies with low D/E survive market downturns better.
- **Return on Equity (ROE)**: A high and consistent ROE indicates efficient management and a competitive moat.
- **Historical Multiples**: Compare current P/E or EV/EBITDA to the company's 5-year and 10-year median. Reversion to the mean is a powerful predictive force.

## 3. The "Next Big Stock" Checklist

Before predicting a major move, verify these "catalysts":
1. **Industry Tailwinds**: Is the company in a growing sector (e.g., AI, Green Energy, Biotech)?
2. **Insider Buying**: Are executives buying their own stock? (Strong historical signal).
3. **Institutional Accumulation**: Is "Smart Money" (hedge funds, pensions) increasing their positions?
4. **Competitive Moat**: Does the company have a unique product, brand, or cost advantage that protects its margins?
